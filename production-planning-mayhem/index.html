How the model works

Industry-based mechanics, simplified for gameplay.

Water balance and storage

Every storage asset follows continuity at each 30-minute step:

V(t+Δt) = V(t) + [inflow − demand − leakage − transfer] × Δt

No water is invented. If the available volume is insufficient, town deliveries are proportionally curtailed.

Demand and daily peaks

Domestic use is based on population × 140 litres per person per day, plus fictional non-household demand. A normalised 48-point profile creates morning and evening peaks. Each stage adds weather, event and forecast-error multipliers.

Pressure and hydraulic loss

Pressure head is storage water-surface elevation minus town elevation and a flow-dependent headloss term. Delivered demand reduces when pressure is low. The game flags the UK Guaranteed Standards Scheme threshold of 7 metres static head.

Leakage and water age

Background leakage increases with pressure and burst events add a separate loss. Water age is tracked as a completely mixed storage approximation; high age reduces the quality score.

What is deliberately simplified

This is an educational operational simulator, not an engineering design model. It does not solve a full looped pipe network, chlorine chemistry, asset-specific pump curves or real company infrastructure. A full professional model would typically use software such as EPANET or an equivalent hydraulic package.

Research basis
  • US EPA EPANET 2.2 capabilities and extended-period simulation.
  • Ofwat guidance on pressure, leakage and water balance.
  • Drinking Water Inspectorate standards for potable-water quality.
  • UK public-demand baseline and peak-demand reporting.
Production Planning Mayhem uses a wholly fictional network. It is designed for learning and entertainment, not live operational decision-making.
